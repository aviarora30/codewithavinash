## Published Notes

### 1. Performance is a People Problem

- **URL**: https://codewithavinash.com/notes/performance-is-a-people-problem/
- **Published**: 26 Jan 2026
- **Tags**: engineering-leadership, ownership, team-performance, leadership, teams
- **Core idea**: Performance problems aren't technical — they're ownership problems. When multiple people are "responsible," no one really is. Blurred ownership kills teams quietly.
- **Key tension**: Teams blame legacy code, changing requirements, or scope creep — but the real root is always a leadership/ownership failure.
- **Memorable line**: "When three people are responsible, no one really is."
- **LinkedIn post**: "I've been part of teams where nothing was technically blocked, yet delivery kept slowing down. Over time, it became clear that the problem wasn't the stack or the codebase, but unclear ownership and decision-making. I captured that experience in a short note here."

### 2. Manufactured Friction: Learning with AI

- **URL**: https://codewithavinash.com/notes/manufactured-friction-learning-with-ai/
- **Published**: 05 Feb 2026
- **Tags**: learning, ai, craftsmanship, depth, deliberate-practice
- **Core idea**: AI makes learning too efficient. The struggle that builds real intuition gets quietly removed. Senior engineers need to reintroduce friction intentionally.
- **Key tension**: AI feels like a learning upgrade, but it removes the resistance that shapes deep skill and judgment.

And some of the most durable lessons I've learned from that friction. Sharing a short reflection on learning with AI, mistakes, and why intentional struggle still matters."

### 3. Learning to Let Go of What Made Me Valuable

- **URL**: https://codewithavinash.com/notes/learning-to-let-go-of-what-made-me-valuable/
- **Published**: 18 Feb 2026
- **Tags**: engineering-leadership, career-growth, mentorship, team-building, delegation
- **Core idea**: Being the go-to problem solver feels like leadership but is actually a bottleneck. Real growth means building a team that doesn't depend on you.
- **Key tension**: Being needed feels like impact. It's actually a system fragility you built yourself.
- **Memorable line**: "The goal is not to be the fastest problem-solver in the room. It is to build a room where problems do not wait for you."
- **LinkedIn post**: "The hardest part of growth isn't learning something new. It's letting go of what made you successful in the first place. I wrote about the shift from individual contribution to building scalable systems and what changed when I stepped back. Would love your thoughts."
- **Impressions**: 1,290 | **Engagements**: 46 | **Engagement rate**: 3.6%

### 4. What I Forgot About Building

- **URL**: https://codewithavinash.com/notes/what-i-forgot-about-building/
- **Published**: 02 Mar 2026
- **Tags**: engineering-leadership, craft, career-growth, reflection, building
- **Core idea**: As you grow into leadership, deep immersive building quietly disappears. A hackathon reminded Avinash how much that focused state still matters.
- **Key tension**: Career growth expands scope and influence but costs you the immersion that made the work feel alive.
- **Memorable line**: "Growth doesn't have to mean complete detachment from the craft."
- **LinkedIn post**: "As careers progress, scope and influence naturally expand. What receives less attention is the gradual distance from hands-on work. The craft that once defined your days becomes something you visit occasionally, if at all. I've been reflecting on what it means to grow without fully disconnecting from that foundation. Perhaps leadership isn't about stepping away entirely, but being intentional about returning when it matters."

### 5. Decision Making in Teams: Context vs Command

- **URL**: https://codewithavinash.com/notes/decision-making-in-teams-context-vs-command/
- **Published**: 20 Mar 2026
- **Tags**: leadership, engineering-leadership, team-dynamics, decision-making, management-lessons
- **Core idea**: Managers who share context alongside decisions build trust organically. Those who only hand down decisions demand trust without earning it.
- **Key tension**: Sharing context feels slower — but it's what makes teams think independently and commit genuinely.
- **Memorable line**: "When people understand the thinking, they start thinking with you."
- **LinkedIn post**: "I've worked with two kinds of managers. One makes decisions and passes them down. The other takes time to explain how and why those decisions were made. Both get the work done. But the difference shows up in how teams engage, how much they own, and what they learn along the way. Teams don't own what they don't understand."
- **Impressions**: 750 | **Engagements**: 42 | **Engagement rate**: 5.6%

### 6. The Issue Was Raised. The Risk Wasn't.

- **URL**: https://codewithavinash.com/notes/the-issue-was-raised-the-risk-wasnt/
- **Published**: 12 Apr 2026
- **Tags**: engineering-leadership, tech-debt, risk-communication, technical-leadership, delivery
- **Core idea**: Technical leads fail not because they stay silent about tech debt, but because they use engineering vocabulary with an audience that decides in risk vocabulary. Raising the issue and communicating the risk are two different things.
- **Key tension**: The lead did everything right — flagged early, stayed transparent, kept asking. Still failed. The failure wasn't courage or timing. It was translational. By the time the language was right, the timing was already wrong.
- **Memorable line**: "Costs stay within the team. Risks travel."

## Writing Style Notes

- Opens with a personal memory or situation, never a definition or generic statement
- Central tension is named explicitly, usually mid-piece
- Ends with honest reflection — not a clean resolution, but a genuine takeaway
- Avoids bullet-point advice dumps (except occasionally at end of piece)
- Uses real examples from actual team situations, anonymized
- Does not moralize — shares perspective, acknowledges uncertainty
